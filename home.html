The COP-30
The United Nations Conference on Climate Change, or COP (Conference of the Parties), stands as the largest global climate event, uniting leaders, scientists, activists, and NGOs to tackle the world's climate challenges. As COP 30 marks its 30th edition since inception in 1995, it serves as a pivotal platform to advance the goals outlined in the Paris Agreement, aiming to limit global temperature rise to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels. This event not only fosters crucial discussions and agreements but also drives momentum for global climate action, emphasizing the urgency of addressing the climate crisis.
